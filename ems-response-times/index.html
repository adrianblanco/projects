Medical emergencies

Although the average attendance time is stabilized, a few blackout areas still remain for serious emergencies

Published October, 2018

By Adrian Blanco

In 2009, when Mayor de Blasio named Michael Flowers, director of analytics of New York, the city started a new program driven by data to detect any kind of inefficiency and tackle it. The Emergency Medical System response time was among them. Using data, they found and reduced the city‚Äôs emergency response times. Although the average response time is stabilized for serious emergencies around nine minutes, there are still a few blackout areas where having a serious emergency could be really problematic.

In total, the number of emergency cases has raised in the last few years. Although the time response remains stable for life threatening emergencies, the consequences that a higher number of cases can produce are specially worrying for those less accessible areas. Among the serious emergencies, there are cardiac conditions, difficulty in breathing, unconsciousness or major burn cases.

An analysis of the data unveils the blackout areas to have an emergency in New York City. According to it, the center of Manhattan and some areas of the Bronx are the worst places to require urgent medical assistance.
